Music-STAR

A Style Translation System for Audio-based Rearrangement

What is Music-STAR?

Music Style Translation has recently gained attention among music processing studies. It aims to generate variations of existing music pieces by altering the style-variant characteristics of the original music piece, while content such as the melody remains unchanged. These alterations involve timbre translation, reharmonization, or music rearrangement. We plan to address music rearrangement, focusing on instrumentation, by processing audio mixtures comprising two instrument tracks. We introduce Music-STAR, a system based on WaveNet autoencoder that can transform the instruments of a multi-track piece into another set of instruments, resulting in a rearranged piece. We evaluate Music-STAR on the StarNet dataset, which include string-piano and vibraphone-clarinet mixtures. We also compare the performance of our model to baseline approaches performed by single-instrument music translation, and separation-translation pipeline. For further information please visit the GitHub repository.
